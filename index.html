Abstract

Recent advances in generative modeling have enabled video generation systems to synthesize high-quality, temporally coherent scenes that simulate realistic world dynamics. However, world models trained purely on real-world videos often exhibit weak interactivityâ€”they persist in previous motion patterns and respond poorly to abrupt action changes. This limitation arises from the inherent bias of real-world datasets, where trajectories such as driving or walking contain few large-magnitude motion transitions. To address this inertia bias, we propose Lyra, a 3D-aware GRPO framework that enhances the action responsiveness and motion accuracy of pre-trained world models through reinforcement learning. By extracting 3D scene attributes from generated videos, we design a 3D-aware reward that enforces geometric and directional consistency between predicted motion and commanded actions, enabling precise control behaviors and encouraging the emergence of spatial intelligence. Moreover, we introduce an action-interface optimization strategy that confines policy updates to the action-conditioning adapter, ensuring stable and efficient fine-tuning while preserving the pre-trained visual fidelity. Without relying on explicit 3D intermediate representations or extra conditioning modules, our method produces sharper, more accurate motion responses and smoother control dynamics, significantly enhancing the interactivity and controllability of world models.

Pipeline

Overview of the proposed Lyra. Lyra enhances a pre-trained video world model through 3D-aware GRPO fine-tuning (left) and action-interface optimization (right). In GRPO training, a pre-trained 3D reconstruction network extracts geometric attributes from generated videos to compute rewards that enforce alignment between predicted motion and commanded actions. Lightweight MLP adapters, inserted before self-attention blocks, inject action signals into motion latents; during GRPO, only these adapters are updated, ensuring stable learning while preserving visual fidelity and temporal coherence.

Future Works

Astra's future work includes releasing full inference pipelines for additional scenarios such as autonomous driving and robotic manipulation, open-sourcing our data processing utilities and training code for broader reproducibility, and developing a higher-resolution version of Astra by integrating more advanced generative backbones such as Wan 2.2.
